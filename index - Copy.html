NUSTECH Academy Module 1 — Automotive Wiring Harness Fundamentals

This foundation course introduces the essentials of wiring harness engineering. Learners explore harness purpose, wire types, terminals, connectors, fuses, relays, clips, grommets, protection methods, special cables, splices, and sealing. By the end, you will understand how harnesses are designed, protected, and validated at a fundamental level—preparing you for advanced OEM workflows.

Foundation Level — No Prior Experience Required

  • Purpose and types of harness

  • Components overview

NUSTECH Academy - Module 2 (Advanced Level)

Complete Wiring Harness Engineering, Validation, Manufacturing & Launch Flow. This sequence is arranged in practical OEM/Tier-1 order so learners understand how a wiring harness moves from design input to release, validation, manufacturing, and vehicle launch.

Industry Expert Track

Chapter 1 — 2D Drawings & Harness Documentation

  • 2D drawing structure: title block, revision block, notes, applicability
  • Wire chart, component chart, BOM, connector details, branch lengths
  • Physical representation of schematic and manufacturing input
  • KBL / VEC basic introduction for digital harness data understanding

Chapter 2 — Wiring Harness Design Process

  • Customer input, 3D data, load matrix, environmental requirements
  • Feasibility study: packaging, manufacturing, cost, risk
  • Circuit design, fuse/relay selection, splice planning
  • Component selection: connectors, terminals, clips, protection, sealing

Chapter 3 — Wiring Harness 3D Routing

  • Concept routing in CAD (CATIA / NX / Creo)
  • Clearance rules, bend radius, fixation strategy, heat zones
  • Slack management, serviceability, segregation rules
  • Zone-based routing strategy: engine bay, cabin, chassis, doors/tailgate

Chapter 4 — DFMEA (Design Failure Mode & Effects Analysis)

  • Identify routing and design risks early
  • Failure modes: chafing, EMI, heat damage, water ingress, slack issues
  • Severity, Occurrence, Detection, RPN / AP thinking
  • Risk controls and linkage to validation planning

Chapter 5 — Fresh Eye Review

  • Independent review by a team not involved in original design
  • Used to catch hidden issues missed by the design team
  • Applied on 3D routing, 2D drawings, packaging and DFMEA
  • Review checklist, issue list, closure tracking, linkage with ECR / DFMEA

Chapter 6 — Drawing Release Process

  • 3D finalization → 2D drawing preparation → internal check
  • ECR / ECN incorporation → final drawing and charts
  • Revision control, CFT review, approvals, PLM release, production release
  • Phase-wise release linkage: NR, DV, PV, SOP

Chapter 7 — BOD & RQA (Validation Tools for Routing)

  • BOD: Build of Design for physical validation
  • RQA: Routing Quality Assessment against OEM requirements
  • Check routing feasibility, lengths, clip positions, heat protection, bend radius
  • Outputs feed back into design correction and release quality

Chapter 8 — ECR / ECN Change Management

  • Engineering Change Request and Engineering Change Notice flow
  • Impact on 3D, 2D, BOM, costing and timeline
  • Feasibility, approval, implementation and revision update
  • DFMEA update and release control after change

Chapter 9 — Special Cables & Modern Vehicle Data Architecture

  • LVDS, Ethernet, Coax, USB, HDMI, CAN communication basics
  • Applications in infotainment, ADAS, cameras, RF and user interfaces
  • Routing, shielding, grounding, connector selection and failure risks
  • Integration of high-speed data cables in harness design

Chapter 10 — Types of Wiring Harness Projects

  • Build to Print (BTP), Build to Model (BTM), Full-Service Supplier (FSS)
  • Design ownership, validation responsibility, risk, profit and customer interaction
  • Commercial model and supplier evolution
  • OEM decision logic for selecting project model

Chapter 11 — VAVE in Wiring Harness

  • Value Analysis / Value Engineering for cost optimization
  • Wire downsizing, routing optimization, connector consolidation, clip reduction
  • Protection optimization, modular harnessing, material substitution, process automation
  • Validation before implementation and VAVE linkage to ECR

Chapter 12 — Wiring Harness Validations

  • Splice validation, terminal validation, sealed connector validation, HST validation
  • Mechanical, electrical, thermal, environmental and durability tests
  • DVP / DVP&R, acceptance criteria, standards awareness
  • Validation link with DFMEA and SOP readiness

Chapter 13 — Wiring Harness Manufacturing Process

  • Raw material preparation, cutting, stripping, crimping, sub-assembly and connector assembly
  • Protection application, continuity testing, final inspection and packaging
  • Process control, PFMEA, defect prevention, traceability
  • Rework / rejection process and quality feedback loop

Chapter 14 — Vehicle Launch Plan (OEM Level)

  • NR → DV → PV → IVER → Non-Saleable → SOP → Saleable
  • Harness role in each phase and validation gates
  • Supplier readiness, PPAP, risk management, cost and timeline impact
  • Launch success metrics and final release discipline
  • Business, cost, quality and engineering all connected in one lifecycle
